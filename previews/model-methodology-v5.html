Deep Dive

Key Inputs

  • AdjOE (adjusted offensive efficiency)
  • AdjDE (adjusted defensive efficiency)
  • Tempo (possessions per 40 min)
  • Efficiency margin (AdjOE - AdjDE)

Data Sources

  • KenPom (AdjOE, AdjDE, Tempo)
  • 15-year calibration dataset (2010-2025)

Assumptions

  • Efficiency margin is the best predictor
  • 0.18 logit coeff. stable across eras
  • Season averages = tournament quality

Limitations

  • No style-of-play mismatch modeling
  • Completely blind to injuries
  • Conference tournament noise in ratings

Full Process Detail

01

Pull KenPom data

AdjOE, AdjDE, and Tempo pulled for all 68 tournament teams. Pre-play-in values used for simulation; updated post-play-in for final bracket.

02

Compute win probabilities

For each possible matchup, a logistic regression formula with a 0.18 coefficient converts the efficiency margin differential into a win probability.

03

Run 10,000 simulations

The full 63-game bracket is simulated 10,000 times. Each simulation is independent. Win probabilities remain fixed across all simulations.

04

Aggregate most-frequent outcomes

For each game slot, the team that advanced most often across simulations is selected. The champion is the team that won the most simulated tournaments.

05

Lock March 19

All 63 picks finalized after the First Four. ESPN Tournament Challenge bracket filled out as timestamp proof.
