Every year, millions of people fill out brackets based on vibes, mascots, and which school a coworker went to. The Scout is built to do better — systematically, at scale, without the bias.

## The Core Idea

The Scout treats every first-round matchup the way a real scout would: watch the film, look at the context, weigh the intangibles. The difference is it can do this for all 32 first-round games simultaneously, in about 90 seconds.

It's powered by a large language model (Claude) fed a structured prompt for each matchup that includes:

- **Coaching tenure & tournament experience** — first-year coaches historically underperform seeds
- **Roster age distribution** — senior-heavy teams close out games; freshman-heavy teams crack under pressure
- **Injury & availability reports** — scraped from beat reporters and official injury designations
- **Recent momentum** — last 10 games, conference tournament results
- **Travel distance & rest days** — a #12 seed flying cross-country on 2 days rest is a different team
- **Clutch performance** — how teams perform in games decided by 5 points or fewer

## How the Prompt Works

Rather than asking the model to "predict a winner," we give it a structured analyst role:

```
You are a senior tournament scout. Analyze this matchup and provide:
1. A 3-sentence scouting report on each team
2. Three specific factors that favor each team
3. One X-factor that could decide the game
4. A confidence-weighted pick with reasoning

Teams: [TEAM_A] vs [TEAM_B]
Context: [structured data dump]
```

The model returns structured JSON that feeds directly into the bracket.

## Why LLMs for This?

Efficiency ratings and seed-based models already exist. The Scout's edge is **contextual synthesis** — the ability to understand that a #5 seed is actually playing like a #2 seed when their All-American point guard returned from injury last week, their coach has a 7-2 record in close tournament games, and they're playing 40 miles from campus.

No regression captures that. The Scout does.

## Limitations

- Hallucination risk on specific player stats (mitigated by structured data injection)
- LLM "preferences" can bias toward brand-name programs — we prompt against this
- No live updating during the tournament (picks locked March 19)
