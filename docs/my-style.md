# Style Guide – March Madness + Portfolio + Conversion (70% Fast. 30% Explain. 100% Compelling.)

## Three Layers

### Layer 1: March Madness Tool (70%)
**Speed first.** Users want info and to use the tool.
- No introductions. Skip straight to data.
- Scannable, minimal.
- Every word earns its place.

Example: "Your bracket. Live scores."

---

### Layer 2: Portfolio/Explainer (20%)
**Show your thinking.** Prove you know your stuff.
- Explain *why* you built it or made a choice.
- Casual but competent.
- Short paragraphs. Clear progression.

Example: "Built with Next.js and real-time API polling. Updates every 30 seconds."

---

### Layer 3: Conversion/Audience (10%)
**Make it worth following.** Not salesy, just authentic.
- Share the project's coolness genuinely.
- Give people a reason to follow/stay connected.
- Make sharing feel natural, not forced.
- Point to other work without being pushy.

Examples:
- "Built this over a weekend. There's more experiments on my GitHub."
- "If you liked this, check out [other project]."
- "Built this to learn real-time data. DM if you want to chat about it."

---

## Core Rules (All Layers)

- No corporate speak or marketing fluff.
- Authentic over polished.
- No em-dashes. Use periods.
- Direct and honest.

## Copy Examples by Layer

**MARCH MADNESS (Fast):**
"Track your bracket. See live updates."

**PORTFOLIO (Explain):**
"Built with Next.js and real-time API polling. Kept it simple so it could refresh every 30 seconds without blowing through API limits."

**CONVERSION (Compelling):**
"Built this over a weekend to learn real-time data pipelines. Pretty happy with how it turned out. If you dig this kind of stuff, I have more projects—check them out."

---

## Conversion Moments (Without Being Salesy)

**CTA Examples that feel natural:**
- "Follow on GitHub for more experiments like this."
- "I share projects and breakdowns on Twitter. Worth a follow."
- "Built this to explore [technical thing]. Check out the code if you want to see how I tackled [specific problem]."
- "Like this? I built [other thing] too."

**NOT:**
- "Subscribe to my newsletter for updates!"
- "Sign up below to stay in the loop."
- "Don't miss out—follow now."

**Social/Share buttons:**
- Keep them visible but not pushy.
- Pair with a light reason: "Share your bracket with friends." (not "Share this to help us grow!")

---

## Positioning This Project

**What you're really selling:** That you build cool stuff and know your way around a problem.

**How to show it:**
- Let the tool speak for itself (it's genuinely useful).
- Explain your choices (shows technical depth).
- Be honest about what it took or what you learned.
- Point people to your other work (shows range).

**Tone:**
- Proud but not cocky.
- "I built this and it works well" not "This is revolutionary."
- Genuine enthusiasm, not hype.

---

## Button Text & CTAs

**Tool buttons (fast mode):**
"Save", "Share bracket", "Pick winner"

**Conversion CTAs (audience mode):**
- "See my other projects"
- "Follow on GitHub"
- "Join my email list" (only if you have a reason: "Get updates when I ship new projects")
- "Share your bracket"

**All should be short and action-oriented.**

---

## When in Doubt

**Fast mode:** Can they understand it in 2 seconds?
**Portfolio mode:** Does this show I know what I'm doing?
**Conversion mode:** Would I genuinely say this about my own work?
