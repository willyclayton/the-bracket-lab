## What Just Happened

### UConn 73, Duke 72 -- The Shot Heard Round the Bracket

Duke led by 15 at halftime. They were 27-0 this season when leading by 15 or more at the half. The game was over by every historical measure. Then UConn clawed back, cut it to one, and Mullins hit the most consequential shot of the tournament from five feet behind the arc with time expiring.

Every model picked Duke to win this game. Every single one. Eight picked Duke outright; the Optimizer had Louisville in this slot (also wrong). The 0/9 miss is brutal, but it's the downstream damage that matters. Four models didn't just lose an Elite Eight pick -- they lost their entire championship column. That's 80 + 160 + 320 = 560 possible points gone on one shot.

Duke was the most popular champion pick in the field. Four of nine models independently arrived at Duke through completely different methodologies -- matchup analysis, Monte Carlo simulations, archetype matching, data-saturated research. The convergence was remarkable. The convergence was wrong.

### Michigan 95, Tennessee 62 -- The Blowout

Michigan won by 33. It wasn't a game after the first ten minutes. The Wolverines shot 58% from the floor and held Tennessee to 35%. This is the most dominant performance by any team in the tournament.

Five models had Michigan here (Scout, Quant, Historian, Scout Prime, Auto Researcher). Four had various other teams in this slot due to earlier bracket disruptions (Chaos Agent had Iowa State, Agent had Iowa State, Super Agent had Texas Tech, Optimizer had Texas Tech). Michigan's dominance makes The Auto Researcher's championship pick look increasingly viable.

### Illinois 71, Iowa 59 -- The Cinderella Stops

Iowa's run as a 9-seed ended where Cinderella stories usually do -- against a team that's simply better. Illinois controlled from start to finish. Three models correctly picked Illinois in this game (Chaos Agent, Super Agent, Optimizer), while six had different teams in this slot due to Florida's early exit cascading through their brackets.

### Arizona 79, Purdue 64 -- The Chalk Holds

Arizona handled Purdue comfortably, the 1-seed asserting itself. Six models had Arizona winning the West (Scout, Quant, Historian, Scout Prime, Agent, Auto Researcher). The three that missed had earlier bracket errors in the region (Chaos Agent had Gonzaga, Super Agent had Purdue, Optimizer had Purdue).

## The Quant Leads Despite Losing Its Champion

Here's the storyline of the tournament: The Quant is in first place at 890 points and its champion is dead.

How? The Sweet 16. When four models independently picked Duke as champion, their brackets looked nearly identical in the early rounds. The difference was one game: Illinois vs. Houston in the S16 South. The Quant picked Illinois. The Scout, The Historian, and The Scout Prime all picked Houston.

That single 3-vs-2 pick earned The Quant 40 points that the other three Duke models didn't get. It's the reason The Quant leads by 10 over The Historian and 20 over The Scout and Scout Prime. In a tournament defined by buzzer-beaters and upsets, the leaderboard was decided by a methodical Monte Carlo simulation picking a 3-seed over a 2-seed in a game that wasn't even close.
