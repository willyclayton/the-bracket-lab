Option 2 — Two-Column Overview

The Quant runs the tournament 10,000 times. The bracket is the most probable path through all those simulations. No narratives, no gut calls — just calibrated win probabilities derived from efficiency data, iterated at scale.

Process

01

Pull KenPom data

AdjOE, AdjDE, and Tempo for all 68 tournament teams.

02

Compute win probabilities

Logistic regression (0.18 coefficient) on efficiency margin differential.

03

Run 10,000 simulations

Full 63-game bracket simulated independently.

04

Aggregate most-frequent outcomes

Team that advanced most often is selected per slot.

05

Lock March 19

ESPN bracket filled out as timestamp proof.

Key Inputs

  • AdjOE (adjusted offensive efficiency)
  • AdjDE (adjusted defensive efficiency)
  • Tempo (possessions per 40 min)
  • Efficiency margin (primary signal)

Data Sources

  • KenPom (AdjOE, AdjDE, Tempo)
  • 15-year calibration dataset (2010-2025)

Limitations

  • No style-of-play mismatch modeling
  • Completely blind to injuries
  • Conference tournament noise in late ratings
