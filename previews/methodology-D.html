Core Philosophy

The Scout treats every matchup the way a real analyst would. Where efficiency models see numbers, it sees context: the injured point guard, the road-weary roster, the first-year coach about to crack.

Key Inputs
  • Coaching experience — tournament wins, Final Four appearances, tenure
  • Roster age — senior vs. freshman distribution
  • Injury / availability — beat reports + official designations
  • Clutch performance — games decided by ≤5 pts
  • Momentum — last 10 games, conference tournament
  • Travel and rest — distance, days since last game
Strengths
  • Captures intangibles pure efficiency models miss entirely
  • Contextual synthesis across 6 factors simultaneously
  • No normalization required — model understands magnitude natively

Data Sources
  • KenPom — efficiency ratings, 2026 season
  • Sports Reference — coaching records and tournament history
  • Beat reporter injury coverage
  • Official injury designations
Key Assumptions
  • LLM contextual synthesis outperforms regression on the same inputs
  • Coaching experience predicts consistently across eras
  • Injury data 48h before lock is sufficiently current
Limitations
  • Hallucination risk on stats — mitigated by injecting verified data
  • Tendency to favor brand-name programs — prompted against, imperfectly
  • No live updates after March 19
