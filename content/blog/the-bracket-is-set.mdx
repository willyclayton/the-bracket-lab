## What the Models See

Six models, six different lenses on the same 68 teams.

Three of them -- The Scout, The Quant, and The Historian -- landed on **Duke** as their champion. When a matchup analyst, a Monte Carlo simulator, and an archetype engine all converge on the same pick through completely different methodologies, that's worth paying attention to. It doesn't mean they're right. But the signal is hard to ignore.

**The Chaos Agent** went with **Gonzaga**. This model is engineered to find vulnerability in favorites and reward teams with upset potential. Gonzaga as champion means its system sees a path that the chalk doesn't.

**The Optimizer** picked **Illinois**. Remember -- this model doesn't optimize for accuracy. It maximizes expected ESPN bracket pool points. Illinois as champion means the expected-value math says that's the pick worth the most points given the scoring structure.

**The Auto Researcher** landed on **Arizona** after backtesting 21 different bracket strategies across 15 years of tournament data. Its ML ensemble and Monte Carlo optimizer settled on a bracket it believes maximizes expected performance.
