Tab: Methodology (long-form)

The Scout uses large language models to analyze each matchup the way a coaching staff would break down film. It evaluates tempo, defensive efficiency, key player matchups, coaching tendencies, and situational factors like tournament experience. Each game gets a structured analysis prompt, and the model picks the team with the stronger overall matchup profile.

Key insight: The Scout weighs coaching tournament experience more heavily in early rounds, where preparation and scheme adjustments often decide close games.

Unlike pure statistical models, The Scout incorporates qualitative factors that are difficult to quantify: locker room chemistry signals, coaching track records in high-pressure games, and how a team's playing style historically performs in the tournament format.

Each matchup prompt includes team statistics, recent performance trends, head-to-head history (if available), and contextual factors like travel distance and rest days. The LLM synthesizes these inputs and returns a structured pick with confidence level and reasoning.
