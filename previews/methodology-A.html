Core Philosophy

The Scout treats every matchup the way a real analyst would — structured data in, contextual judgment out. Where efficiency models see numbers, The Scout sees context: the injured point guard, the road-weary roster, the first-year coach about to crack.

Key Inputs
  • Coaching experience — tournament wins, Final Four appearances, tenure
  • Roster age — senior-heavy vs. freshman-heavy distribution
  • Injury and availability — beat reporter coverage + official designations
  • Clutch performance — record in games decided by 5 points or fewer
  • Momentum — last 10 games, conference tournament trajectory
  • Travel and rest — distance to game site, days since last game
Strengths
  • Captures intangibles that pure efficiency models miss entirely
  • Contextual synthesis — a single prompt can weigh 6 factors simultaneously
  • No data normalization required — the model understands magnitude natively

Methodology The Actual Build
The Process
01
Pull team dataFor each team: coaching record, roster age breakdown, recent injury log, last-10 results, travel schedule.
02
Build matchup context objectPair team A vs. team B data into a structured JSON context block for each of the 32 first-round games.
03
Run structured analyst promptFeed each context block to Claude with a specific analyst role. Model returns scouting report + confidence-weighted pick in JSON.
04
Advance bracket round by roundWinning teams from Round 1 become the context for Round 2 matchups. Repeat through the championship.
05
Lock picks March 19Final bracket exported to JSON. ESPN Tournament Challenge entry submitted as timestamp proof.
Data Sources
  • KenPom efficiency ratings (2026 season through conference tournaments)
  • Sports Reference coaching records and tournament history
  • Beat reporter injury coverage (ESPN, team beat writers)
  • Official injury designations where available
Key Assumptions
  • LLM contextual synthesis produces better picks than pure regression on the same inputs
  • Coaching experience is a consistent predictor across eras (pre-2021 data included)
  • Injury data captured 48 hours before picks lock is sufficiently current
Limitations
  • Hallucination risk on specific player stats — mitigated by injecting verified data rather than asking model to recall
  • LLM tendency to favor brand-name programs — explicitly prompted against, imperfectly corrected
  • No live updates after March 19 — late injuries unaccounted for
Example — Duke (#5) vs. Oregon (#12), South
SOUTH REGION · ROUND OF 64 · #5 DUKE vs. #12 OREGON
Pick: Oregon — 61% confidence
Duke has 3 freshman starters. Oregon is senior-led with a 9-1 clutch record. The gap in coaching tournament experience favors Duke (Scheyer 2 years vs. Altman 16 years), but Oregon's pace control and road experience outweigh it. The Scout flags this as a live upset.
